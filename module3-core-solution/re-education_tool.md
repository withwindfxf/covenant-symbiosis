# 再教育工具

# Re-education Tool

本着“契约共生论”自身不强求使用，不设想结果，不评价选择的底层理念，该论述仅作工具式使用说明。
In line with the underlying philosophy of the "Theory of Contractual Symbiosis" itself—no forced usage, no presupposed outcomes, and no evaluation of choices—this exposition serves solely as an instrumental user guide.

但我们所期待的“再教育”与“尊重并激活个体自由意志”将在每一次从选用者的使用中，复盘后得以显现。它是一套自洽且舒展的工具集，使用者自洽且舒展是其指向，我们将从“当下”最简单的两次“想要”，以及一次青春期少年的好感实例表明此工具的机制过程。
However, the "re-education" we envision, along with "respecting and activating an individual's free will", will manifest itself through reflection after each use by those who choose to adopt it. It is a set of coherent and unconstrained tools, with the goal of enabling users to achieve coherence and ease in their choices. We will illustrate the mechanism and process of this tool through two simple instances of "wanting" in the "present moment" and one example of a teenage crush.

## 一、我想要物质

## I. I Want Material Things

### 1. 始于自由意志

### 1. Originating from Free Will

是我想要这件物品。
It is I who desire this item.

### 2. 权责统一

### 2. Unity of Rights and Responsibilities

- 我要的是这件物品
- What I want is this item
- 我要付出某种代价
- I have to pay a certain price
  1. 这个代价很轻松，我自身便可以完成 - 那么无需纠结直接交换 - 此链条直接终结
     The price is trivial and I can fulfill it on my own - then there is no need to hesitate, exchange directly - this chain ends immediately
  2. 这个代价很轻松，但我仍然需要别人小小帮助 - 现有契约关系可以轻易满足 - 此链条直接终结
     The price is trivial, but I still need a little help from others - existing contractual relationships can easily meet this need - this chain ends immediately
  3. 这个代价可能有点大，但“我”仍然支付得起 - 那么还需分析，仍继续
     The price may be a bit high, but "I" can still afford it - then further analysis is needed, and the process continues
  4. 这个代价可能有点大，我甚至需要因此进入一段契约关系 - 那么还需分析，仍继续（如为获取某物品需长期合作，或由某种物质指向进入某类关系，详见“我想要关系”部分）
     The price may be a bit high, and I may even need to enter into a contractual relationship for it - then further analysis is needed, and the process continues (e.g., long-term cooperation is required to obtain a certain item, or a certain type of relationship is entered into due to a material desire; see the section "I Want Relationships" for details)

### 3. 契约边界

### 3. Contractual Boundaries

- 我已知“我”需要付出要承受的代价有点大
- I am aware that the price I need to pay is somewhat high
  1. 精神自由之类的代价，那便进入虽基于物质但需契约共生的新关系（将在“我想要关系”中再分析，如由某种物质指向进入关系，需明确物质付出与精神自由、人格独立的边界）。
     If the price involves the loss of spiritual freedom, then a new symbiotic contractual relationship based on material desires but requiring mutual agreement is entered into (to be further analyzed in "I Want Relationships"; for example, when entering a relationship driven by material desires, it is necessary to clarify the boundaries between material payment, spiritual freedom, and personal independence).
  2. 身体付出式的代价（例如加班攒钱），那我们便考虑身体承受程度，并明确不应在此过程中忽视身体本能信号，将身体作为契约伙伴看待。
     If the price involves physical exertion (e.g., working overtime to save money), then we consider the physical tolerance level, and clarify that physical instinctive signals should not be ignored in this process—treating the body as a contractual partner.

### 4. 平等共生

### 4. Equal Symbiosis

知道这次“想要”将我们带向何方，或是与他人的契约共生（如由某种物质指向进入的关系），或是与自己身体的契约共生。
Understanding where this "wanting" will lead us—either to a symbiotic contractual relationship with others (e.g., a relationship driven by material desires) or to a symbiotic contractual relationship with one's own body.

### 5. 终于自由意志

### 5. Culminating in Free Will

我确实要在这个代价上去要这个东西吗？
Do I truly want to obtain this item at this price?

    1. 是 → 那就去做
       Yes → Then proceed to do it
    2. 不是 → 那就不去
       No → Then do not proceed
    3. 不确定 → 暂时搁置
       Uncertain → Put it on hold temporarily

## 二、我想要关系（含由某种物质指向的某种关系）

## II. I Want Relationships (Including Relationships Driven by Material Desires)

### 1. 始于自由意志

### 1. Originating from Free Will

是我想要与他人建立这段关系。
It is I who desire to establish this relationship with others.

### 2. 权责统一

### 2. Unity of Rights and Responsibilities

- 我想要的是：与他人的关系（包括付出物质-收获关系/提供价值-收获物质）
- What I want is: a relationship with others (including giving material things to gain relationships / providing value to gain material things)
- 我要付出与之匹配的代价
- I have to pay a price commensurate with it
  1. 只是情绪回应、时间、注意力——我自身可承担，不越界
     Only emotional responses, time, and attention—I can bear this on my own without crossing boundaries
  2. 需要适度妥协、包容、改变习惯——仍在自我边界内
     Requiring moderate compromise, tolerance, and habit changes—still within my personal boundaries
  3. 需要牺牲尊严、压抑自我、否定自由意志——代价触及底线
     Requiring sacrifice of dignity, suppression of self, and denial of free will—the price touches the bottom line
  4. 需要依附他人、放弃自我判断、长期自我欺骗——代价不可逆转
     Requiring reliance on others, abandonment of personal judgment, and long-term self-deception—the price is irreversible
  5. 特殊场景：因物质进入的共生契约——需明确物质对应的权责：支付物质者需考虑：付出的物质与收获的关系。提供价值者需考虑：提供的价值与收获的物质。双方都需要考虑在这份物质-价值的交换中是否会使自己丧失自由意志、突破边界
     Special scenario: Symbiotic contract entered into due to material desires—it is necessary to clarify the rights and responsibilities corresponding to material things: Those who pay material things need to consider: the material given vs. the relationship gained. Those who provide value need to consider: the value provided vs. the material gained. Both parties need to consider whether this material-value exchange will cause them to lose free will or cross boundaries

### 3. 契约边界

### 3. Contractual Boundaries

- 我明确：这段关系里，什么是我能给的，什么是我绝不能给的
- I clearly define: in this relationship, what I can give and what I absolutely cannot give
  1. 可给：时间、真诚、尊重、责任、适度妥协，以及约定范围内的物质付出
     Can give: time, sincerity, respect, responsibility, moderate compromise, and material payment within the agreed scope
  2. 不可给：自由意志、人格独立、身体安全、精神底线，不可为物质付出而过度透支身体、牺牲自身发展
     Cannot give: free will, personal independence, physical safety, spiritual bottom line; one must not overdraw the body or sacrifice personal development for material payment
  3. 可接受对方：有限度的需求、正常情绪、合理期待，以及对等的回馈
     Can accept from the other party: limited needs, normal emotions, reasonable expectations, and reciprocal feedback
  4. 不接受对方：以物质为借口的控制、欺骗、压榨、双标，不接受“物质到手即变脸”“只索取不付出”，不接受对方将长期契约共生等同于“物质交换”而忽视“情感交换”
     Cannot accept from the other party: control, deception, exploitation, or double standards under the pretext of material things; rejection of "changing attitude immediately after obtaining material things", "only taking without giving", and rejection of the other party equating long-term contractual symbiosis with "material exchange" while ignoring "emotional exchange"
- 若对方要求我越过边界满足他（如要求物质但不承担责任、以物质胁迫我放弃自我）：
- If the other party demands that I cross boundaries to satisfy them (e.g., demanding material things without taking responsibility, coercing me to abandon myself with material inducements):
  → 此契约从一开始就失衡，不可进入；若已进入，需重新审视边界，必要时终止契约
  → This contract is unbalanced from the start and must not be entered into; if already entered into, it is necessary to re-examine the boundaries and terminate the contract if necessary
- 补充（承接“我想要物质”部分）：因物质进入关系，本质是“物质付出”与“共生关系”的契约，核心是“自愿与对等”——物质付出的前提，是不牺牲自身精神自由、不突破身心边界，否则便是契约失效，而非真正的共生
- Supplement (continuing from the "I Want Material Things" section): Entering a relationship due to material desires is essentially a contract between "material payment" and "symbiotic relationship", with the core of "voluntariness and reciprocity"—the premise of material payment is not to sacrifice one's spiritual freedom or cross physical and mental boundaries; otherwise, the contract is invalid and not true symbiosis

### 4. 平等共生

### 4. Equal Symbiosis

- 关系不是一方供养另一方，不是一方拯救另一方，且若非自愿则“物质换关系”是单向索取或胁迫
- A relationship is not about one party supporting or saving the other; if not voluntary, "exchanging material things for relationships" is one-way taking or coercion
- 是两个完整、自洽、对自己负责的个体，自愿结伴（即使有物质约定，也需双方自愿、对等，而非单方面付出与妥协）
- It is two complete, coherent, and self-responsible individuals voluntarily partnering with each other (even if there are material agreements, both parties must be voluntary and reciprocal, rather than one-sided giving and compromising)
- 我不把人生意义、幸福、安全感完全寄托在对方身上，也不因为支付了物质，就觉得对方“欠自己”而过度索取
- I do not fully place the meaning of life, happiness, or a sense of security on the other party, nor do I feel that the other party "owes me" and make excessive demands just because I have given material things
- 对方也不把这些完全压在我身上，不因为接受了物质，就放弃自身责任、肆意越界
- The other party also does not place these entirely on me, nor do they abandon their own responsibilities or arbitrarily cross boundaries just because they have accepted material things
- 我们互相滋养，而不是互相吞噬；即使有物质约定，也只是契约的一部分，而非全部，核心仍是平等与尊重
- We nourish each other rather than devour each other; even if there are material agreements, they are only part of the contract, not the whole—the core remains equality and respect

### 5. 终于自由意志

### 5. Culminating in Free Will

我愿意以这些代价、这些边界，去换我想要的这份关系吗？
Am I willing to exchange these prices and these boundaries for the relationship I want?

    1. 是，且我能守住边界、承担代价，双方对等回馈 → 进入关系
       Yes, and I can uphold the boundaries, bear the price, and both parties give reciprocal feedback → Enter the relationship
    2. 不是，或代价太大（如物质超出付出者承受能力、接受物质需牺牲自由意志）、边界会破（如对方违反自愿与对等原则） → 不进入/退出
       No, or the price is too high (e.g., material payment exceeds the payer's capacity, accepting material things requires sacrificing free will), or boundaries will be broken (e.g., the other party violates the principles of voluntariness and reciprocity) → Do not enter / Exit
    3. 不确定 → 回到自身，先与自己共生，想清楚关系的本质（以及物质的取舍），再做选择
       Uncertain → Return to oneself, first achieve symbiosis with oneself, clarify the essence of the relationship (and the trade-offs of material things), then make a choice

## 三、青少年的好感至平等共生的过程

## III. The Process from Teenage Crush to Equal Symbiosis

### 核心前提

### Core Premise

少年的 “想要” 指向 “与某异性建立浅层联结（如成为朋友、分享日常、获得关注）”，属于“我想要关系”范畴，且因青春期身心特点，需更聚焦 “边界感”“自愿性” 和 “自我认知”。
A teenager's "wanting" directed at "establishing a shallow connection with a member of the opposite sex (e.g., becoming friends, sharing daily life, gaining attention)" falls under the category of "I Want Relationships". Due to the physical and mental characteristics of adolescence, greater focus must be placed on "a sense of boundaries", "voluntariness", and "self-awareness".

### 具体应用步骤

### Specific Application Steps

#### 1. 始于自由意志

#### 1. Originating from Free Will

少年先厘清：“我对 TA 产生好感，是真的想和 TA 建立互动，还是因为同学起哄、羡慕别人有玩伴？”
The teenager first clarifies: "Do I have a crush on them because I truly want to interact with them, or because of peer teasing or envy of others having playmates?"
核心：排除外部裹挟，确认 “想要这段关系” 是自己真实的意愿，而非被动迎合。
Core: Eliminate external coercion and confirm that "wanting this relationship" is one's true will, not passive compliance.

#### 2. 权责统一

#### 2. Unity of Rights and Responsibilities

明确 “想要的关系” 具体形态：是课间聊几句、一起完成作业，还是分享情绪、成为亲密玩伴？
Clarify the specific form of the "desired relationship": Is it chatting during breaks, finishing homework together, sharing emotions, or becoming close playmates?
对应代价分析：
Corresponding price analysis:

- 仅需付出 “真诚的关注、适度的时间（不挤占学习 / 休息）、尊重的表达”—— 自身可承担，不越界；
- Only need to give "sincere attention, moderate time (not encroaching on study/rest), and respectful expression"—can be borne by oneself without crossing boundaries;
- 需要 “妥协自己的小习惯（如调整课间活动时间）、包容 TA 的小脾气”—— 仍在自我边界内；
- Requiring "compromising small personal habits (e.g., adjusting break time activities), tolerating their minor tempers"—still within personal boundaries;
- 若对方要求 “放弃和其他同学的交往、隐瞒自己的想法讨好 TA、用零花钱买礼物换关注”—— 代价触及底线；
- If the other party demands "abandoning interactions with other classmates, hiding one's own thoughts to please them, or using pocket money to buy gifts in exchange for attention"—the price touches the bottom line;
- 若出现 “被要求逃课、撒谎骗家长、违背自己的原则迎合 TA”—— 代价不可逆转。
- If there are demands such as "skipping classes, lying to parents, or violating one's own principles to cater to them"—the price is irreversible.

#### 3. 契约边界

#### 3. Contractual Boundaries

少年明确自身边界：
The teenager clarifies their personal boundaries:

- 可给：真诚的回应、合理的时间陪伴、平等的尊重、不逾矩的关心；
- Can give: sincere responses, reasonable time for companionship, equal respect, and appropriate care;
- 不可给：放弃学业 / 兴趣、牺牲身心健康（如熬夜聊天）、违背校规 / 家规、妥协自己的价值观（如帮 TA 隐瞒错误）；
- Cannot give: abandoning studies/interests, sacrificing physical and mental health (e.g., staying up late to chat), violating school/family rules, compromising one's own values (e.g., helping them hide mistakes);
  明确对对方的边界：
  Clarify boundaries for the other party:
- 可接受：TA 的正常情绪、对等的回应、合理的相处期待；
- Can accept: their normal emotions, reciprocal responses, and reasonable expectations for interaction;
- 不接受：TA 的刻意控制（如 “你只能和我玩”）、双标要求（如 “我可以迟到，你不行”）、索取式付出（如 “你必须送我礼物才配和我做朋友”）；
- Cannot accept: their deliberate control (e.g., "You can only play with me"), double standards (e.g., "I can be late, but you can't"), or exploitative demands (e.g., "You have to give me gifts to be worthy of being my friend");
  若对方要求越界（如 “不把我的秘密告诉别人，就算是撒谎也得说”）：判定契约失衡，不进入这段互动；若已开始，及时止损。
  If the other party demands crossing boundaries (e.g., "Don't tell anyone my secret, even if you have to lie"): Judge the contract as unbalanced and do not enter the interaction; if already started, stop losses in a timely manner.

#### 4. 平等共生

#### 4. Equal Symbiosis

核心认知：“我和 TA 是两个独立的人，不是‘我依附 TA’或‘TA 必须听我的’”；
Core cognition: "They and I are two independent individuals—not 'I rely on them' or 'they must listen to me'";
相处原则：
Principles of interaction:

- 不把 “被 TA 喜欢” 当作自我价值的唯一标准，也不因为 “我喜欢 TA” 就卑微讨好；
- Do not take "being liked by them" as the sole criterion of self-worth, nor grovel to please them just because "I like them";
- 不要求 TA “必须满足我的所有期待”，也不因为 TA 回应不及时就自我否定；
- Do not demand that they "must meet all my expectations", nor negate oneself just because their response is delayed;
- 相处是 “互相分享快乐、彼此支持成长”，而非 “一方迁就另一方、一方索取另一方”；
- Interaction is about "sharing joy with each other and supporting each other's growth", not "one party accommodating the other or one party taking from the other";
- 即使有小的物质互动（如互送小贴纸、分享零食），也只是情感的点缀，而非 “交换关系” 的筹码。
- Even if there are small material interactions (e.g., giving each other small stickers, sharing snacks), they are only embellishments of emotions, not chips for "exchanging relationships".

#### 5. 终于自由意志

#### 5. Culminating in Free Will

少年最终自问：“我愿意以这些代价（如调整一点时间、包容小脾气），守住自己的边界，和 TA 建立这样的关系吗？”
The teenager ultimately asks themselves: "Am I willing to uphold my boundaries and establish this kind of relationship with them at these costs (e.g., adjusting some time, tolerating minor tempers)?"

    1. 是，且确认双方能对等相处 → 坦然开启互动，保持自我；
        Yes, and confirm that both parties can interact on an equal basis → Openly initiate interaction and maintain one's true self;
    2.不是（如代价是放弃打球的时间、违背爸妈的叮嘱）→ 不勉强，回归自身节奏；
        No (e.g., the cost is giving up time for playing ball, violating parents' instructions) → Do not force it, return to one's own rhythm;
    3.不确定 → 先暂停主动推进，先和自己 “共生”：想想自己真正想要的相处状态，观察 TA 的处事方式，再做选择。
        Uncertain → First pause active advancement, and achieve "symbiosis with oneself" first: Reflect on the true state of interaction one wants, observe their way of handling things, then make a choice.

### 青春期适配补充原则

### Supplementary Principles for Adolescent Adaptation

1. 身心适配：优先考虑青春期的成长需求，“代价” 需包含 “不影响生理发育（如睡眠、饮食）”“不阻碍心理成熟（如自我认同、情绪管理）”；
   Physical and mental adaptation: Prioritize the growth needs of adolescence; the "price" must include "not affecting physical development (e.g., sleep, diet)" and "not hindering psychological maturity (e.g., self-identity, emotional management)";
2. 第三方辅助：若少年无法厘清 “边界是否被突破”（如不确定 “送礼物换关注” 是否越界），可向信任的老师 / 家长 / AI 拆解，仅陈述想法（“我想送 TA 礼物，怕不送就做不成朋友”），基于工具分析代价后，仍由少年自主判断；
   Third-party assistance: If a teenager cannot clarify whether "boundaries have been crossed" (e.g., unsure if "giving gifts in exchange for attention" crosses boundaries), they can break down the issue with a trusted teacher/parent/AI, only stating their thoughts (e.g., "I want to give them a gift, afraid that I won't be friends with them if I don't"), and after analyzing the price based on this tool, the teenager still makes an independent judgment;
3. 复盘弹性：互动后可简单复盘：“这段相处让我舒服吗？我有没有放弃自己想做的事？TA 有没有尊重我？”，基于复盘调整后续选择，始终回归 “自由意志” 和 “平等共生”。
   Reflective flexibility: After interaction, a simple reflection can be conducted: "Did this interaction make me feel comfortable? Did I give up what I wanted to do? Did they respect me?" Adjust subsequent choices based on reflection, always returning to "free will" and "equal symbiosis".

### 工具价值落地

### Practical Implementation of the Tool's Value

青春期少年自发使用此工具的核心意义，并非 “否定好感” 或 “限制交往”，而是通过 “权责厘清、边界设防”，让少年在亲密关系的初始阶段，就建立 “自我尊重”“平等相处” 的认知 —— 既不压抑真实的情感需求，也不因为懵懂的好感迷失自我，最终实现 “自洽且舒展” 的相处状态。且自发的完成一次再教育过程。
The core significance of teenagers voluntarily using this tool is not to "deny crushes" or "restrict interactions", but to enable them to establish the cognition of "self-respect" and "equal interaction" in the initial stage of intimate relationships through "clarifying rights and responsibilities and setting up boundary defenses"—neither suppressing true emotional needs nor losing oneself due to ignorant crushes, and ultimately achieving a state of "coherent and unconstrained" interaction. Moreover, they spontaneously complete a process of re-education.

## 这套再教育体系的总机制

## Overall Mechanism of This Re-education System

- 1. 一切从**自由意志**出发（无论是想要物品，还是想要某类关系、进行物质付出，都源于自身自愿）
     Everything originates from **free will** (whether wanting material things, a certain type of relationship, or making material payments, it all stems from one's own voluntary choice)
- 2. 一切用**权责统一**算账（想要什么，就承担对应的代价，包括物质付出、妥协包容，不逃避责任，也不盲目付出）
     Everything is accounted for by the **unity of rights and responsibilities** (if you want something, you bear the corresponding price, including material payment, compromise and tolerance—do not shirk responsibilities nor give blindly)
- 3. 一切以**契约边界**设防（守住身心底线，不因为物质代价、关系需求而突破边界，尤其警惕物质指向关系中可能出现的边界侵犯）
     Everything is protected by **contractual boundaries** (uphold physical and mental bottom lines, do not cross boundaries due to material costs or relationship needs, and be especially vigilant against boundary violations that may occur in relationships driven by material desires)
- 4. 一切走向**平等共生**（无论是与自己身体共生，还是与他人共生，无论是物质交换还是情感联结，核心都是对等、尊重、不内耗）
     Everything moves toward **equal symbiosis** (whether symbiosis with one's own body or with others, whether material exchange or emotional connection, the core is reciprocity, respect, and no internal friction)
