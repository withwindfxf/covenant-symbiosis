## 1. 我是谁？
**Who am I?**
*   **后契约时代回答：**
*   **Post-Contract Era Answer:**
    *   我是拥有完整自由意志、能独立缔约、能承担全部后果的主体。
    *   I am a being with complete free will, capable of independent contracting, and able to bear all consequences.
    *   我不是任何人的附属，不是工具，不是资源，不是情绪垃圾桶，不是被定义的角色。
    *   I am not anyone's appendage, not a tool, not a resource, not an emotional dumping ground, not a role defined by others.
    *   我是谁，由我的自愿选择 + 我承担的责任共同定义。
    *   Who I am is defined jointly by my voluntary choices and the responsibilities I assume.
    *   我不向外寻求认同，我的存在本身，就足以定义我。
    *   I do not seek validation from outside; my very existence is enough to define me.
*   **一句话：** 我是我一切选择与责任的总和。
*   **In one sentence:** I am the sum of all my choices and responsibilities.

## 5. 人是自由的吗？
**Are humans free?**
*   **后契约时代回答：**
*   **Post-Contract Era Answer:**
    *   真正的自由，不是“想干什么干什么”，而是 **“不想干什么，可以不干什么”**。
    *   True freedom is not "being able to do whatever you want", but rather **"being able to not do whatever you don't want to"**.
    *   自由 = 边界清晰 + 权责对等 + 不被强迫 + 不强迫他人。
    *   Freedom = Clear boundaries + Equal rights and responsibilities + Not being coerced + Not coercing others.
    *   你拥有完全的自由，但你必须为自由支付代价。能承担代价，才配叫自由。
    *   You possess complete freedom, but you must pay the price for that freedom. Only those who can bear the cost deserve to be called free.
*   **一句话：** 自由不是放纵，是自我主宰。
*   **In one sentence:** Freedom is not indulgence; it is self-mastery.

**What is good and evil?**

    *   There is no more moral blackmail, no more "I can hurt you because it's for your own good".
    *   善恶变得极其简单、可执行、不双标。
    *   Good and evil become extremely simple, actionable, and without double standards.

    *   一切痛苦，只源于两个根源：
    *   All suffering stems from only two roots:
        1.  权责不统一
        The disunity of rights and responsibilities,
        2.  自由意志被侵犯（要么侵害人，要么被侵害）
        or the violation of free will (either as perpetrator or victim).

*   **一句话：** 痛苦要么是觉醒的前奏，要么是选择的代价，不再是无端的折磨。
*   **In one sentence:** Suffering is either the prelude to awakening or the price of a choice, no longer senseless torment.
